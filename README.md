THE ROLE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN CYBER SECURITY 
A CAPSTONE PROJECT SUBMITTED TO IDEAS BAZE UNIVERSITY, ABUJA 
ABSTRACT
This research work examines the role of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in enhancing Cyber Security within the Nigerian digital landscape. As Nigeria continues its rapid digital transformation through fintech and e-government services, the frequency and complexity of cyber-attacks such as phishing, ransomware, and identity theft have increased significantly. This study investigates how AI technologies, specifically Machine Learning and automated threat detection, can provide a more robust defense compared to traditional manual security methods. Using a descriptive research design and secondary data sourced from Nigerian and international textbooks, the study reveals that AI offers superior speed, 24/7 monitoring, and predictive capabilities that are essential for protecting financial institutions and government data. However, the study also identifies high implementation costs and poor power infrastructure as major barriers to AI adoption in Nigeria. The research concludes that for Nigeria to secure its digital economy, there must be intentional investment in AI-driven security frameworks. It is recommended that the Nigerian government and private sector collaborate to subsidize AI tools and update educational curricula to include AI-based security training.
Chapter 1 Introduction 
1.1 Background of the Study
In the modern world, the way we live and do business has changed because of the internet. In Nigeria, we have seen a massive shift toward digital services. From the use of the Bank Verification Number (BVN) and NIN to the rise of fintech apps like OPay, Kuda, and Moniepoint, almost every part of our daily life is now online. However, as we move more of our money and information online, the risk of "Cyber-attacks" has also increased.
Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information, or even stealing money from individuals and companies. In Nigeria, many organizations face threats like Phishing (scam emails), Ransomware (locking files for money), and Identity Theft.
The problem is that hackers are becoming more clever. They use automated tools to attack thousands of people at the same time. Human security experts alone can no longer keep up with this speed. This is where Artificial Intelligence (AI) comes in.
AI is a branch of computer science that builds smart machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. In the world of cyber security, AI acts like a "Digital Security Guard" that never sleeps. While a human might get tired or miss a small detail, AI can scan millions of files in seconds to find a hidden virus. It learns from past attacks to predict and stop future ones before they even happen.
According to Ademola (2025), the integration of AI into Nigeria’s digital space is no longer just an option but a necessity to protect our national economy. By using AI, Nigerian banks, government agencies, and small businesses can detect suspicious activities—such as a login attempt from a strange country—and block it immediately.
This study, therefore, looks at how Artificial Intelligence can be used to strengthen our defenses and ensure that our digital future in Nigeria remains safe and secure.
1.2  Statement of the Problem
Despite the many benefits of the internet in Nigeria, cyber-crime has become a major threat to our national development. Every day, thousands of Nigerians and local businesses fall victim to hackers. The problem we are facing can be broken down into three main areas:
1. The Speed of Modern Attacks:
In the past, hackers were individuals who worked slowly. Today, they use "Bots" (automated programs) that can attack a bank’s server thousands of times in one minute. Human security officers in Nigeria cannot work at this speed. By the time a human notices an attack, the damage—such as stolen money or leaked data—has already been done.
2.  The Shortage of Experts:
Nigeria currently has a shortage of highly trained cyber security professionals. Many companies cannot afford to hire a large team of experts to watch their systems 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This leaves many small and medium-sized businesses (SMEs) in Lagos, Abuja, and other cities vulnerable to "Yahoo-Yahoo" (cyber-fraud) and other digital threats.
3.  New and Unknown Threats:
Traditional security software (like basic Antivirus) only recognizes old, known viruses. However, hackers are constantly creating "Zero-day" attacks—these are brand-new viruses that have never been seen before. Because traditional software is not "smart," it fails to stop these new threats, leading to massive losses for Nigerian organizations.
Therefore, there is an urgent need to move away from manual security methods and adopt Artificial Intelligence. This research seeks to address how AI can solve these problems by providing a faster, smarter, and more reliable way to protect our digital space.

1.7 Definition of Terms
To ensure a clear understanding of this project, the following terms are defined as they are used in this context:
• Artificial Intelligence (AI): The ability of a computer or robot to perform tasks usually done by humans, such as learning and problem-solving.
• Cyber Security: The methods used to protect computers, networks, and data from being stolen or damaged by hackers.
• Malware: Short for "Malicious Software." These are harmful programs (like viruses) designed to secretly enter and damage a computer.
• Phishing: A type of online scam where hackers send fake emails or SMS (often looking like they are from a Nigerian bank) to steal passwords or money.
• Machine Learning (ML): A part of AI that allows a system to learn from data and get better at its job over time without being told exactly what to do.
• Algorithm: A set of step-by-step instructions that the computer follows to solve a problem or detect a threat.
• Hacker: A person who uses computers to gain unauthorized access to data or systems.
• Firewall: A digital barrier that controls what goes in and out of a network to keep out hackers.
• Data Breach: An incident where sensitive or protected data is viewed or stolen by someone who is not supposed to see it.

2.2 Theoretical Framework
This is a set of ideas that explains why things happen. 
1. Routine Activity Theory (RAT): This theory (often cited by Nigerian scholars like Adeoye et al., 2025) says that for a crime to happen, there must be a motivated offender (hacker), a suitable target (a bank or person), and the absence of a capable guardian. 
This means AI is the "Capable Guardian" that protects the target.
2. Protection Motivation Theory (PMT): This explains why people and companies choose to protect themselves. They do it when they realize the threat is real and but if they have a tool (like AI) that can actually help them.
2.3 Review of Nigerian Literature
This section connect the global technology of AI to the local reality of Nigeria.
• On National Security: Odinakachukwu et al. (2024) argue that Nigeria faces unique threats like banditry and cyber-crime (Yahoo-Yahoo). They recommend that the Nigerian government should invest heavily in AI to help law enforcement agencies (like the Police and EFCC) track digital criminals.
• On Financial Security: Adedoyin (2023) highlights that Nigerian banks are using AI to look for "patterns" in transactions. For example, if you usually spend money in Ibadan and suddenly your card is used in China, AI detects this anomaly and blocks the account immediately.
• On Challenges: Garba and Bade (2021) noted that even though AI is great, Nigeria faces "Infrastructure Deficits." This means our poor electricity and slow internet make it hard for some companies to run powerful AI security tools.

3.3 System Analysis (How AI Security Works)
 The "Logic" of how an AI security system actually operates.
1. Data Collection: The AI looks at everything happening on a network (emails, logins, file transfers).
2. Pattern Recognition: The AI compares this data to what it has learned. For example, if a staff member in a Kano office usually logs in at 8:00 AM, but suddenly logs in at 3:00 AM from a Russian IP address, the AI flags it.
3. Response: The AI automatically blocks the user or sends an alert to the IT manager.

Chapter 4 System Analysis and Discussion 
 4.1 Analysis of AI Capabilities in Cyber Security
From the research conducted, it was discovered that AI provides three main "superpowers" to cyber security:
1. Threat Detection: AI doesn't wait for a virus to attack. It looks for "bad behavior." For example, if a computer starts encrypting files very fast (a sign of Ransomware), the AI recognizes this pattern and kills the process immediately.
2. Automated Response: In a typical Nigerian office, an IT worker might take 4 hours to notice a hack. AI responds in milliseconds. It can disconnect a hacked computer from the internet before the hacker can steal any data.
3. Predictive Analytics: By looking at data from millions of attacks worldwide, AI can predict which Nigerian sectors (like Fintech or Government) are likely to be targeted next.
4.2 The Nigerian Perspective: Case Studies and Findings
During the study, several key findings were made regarding the Nigerian digital space:
The Banking Sector: Nigerian banks (like Zenith, GTBank, and Access) are the biggest adopters of AI. They use AI Fraud Detection to monitor millions of transactions daily. This is why sometimes we get a call from your bank asking if you truly authorized a transaction—that's AI at work.
The Rise of Fintech: Startups in Lagos are using AI to verify identities. When you take a "selfie" to open an account on a Nigerian app, AI compares that photo with your NIMC/BVN data to ensure you aren't an impersonator.
